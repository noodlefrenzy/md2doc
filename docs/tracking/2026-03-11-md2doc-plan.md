## Key Decisions

- Chose 8 sprints across 4 waves (Foundation, Rich Elements, Style System, Polish) over a flat sprint list because dependency ordering requires sequential waves, but within Wave 2 sprints 3/4/5 are partially parallelizable.
- Chose to place the table auto-sizing prototype gate in Sprint 3 (not earlier) because it depends on the DocxEmitter infrastructure from Sprint 2. The gate runs early in Sprint 3.
- Chose to group Math and Mermaid in the same sprint (Sprint 5) because they share the Playwright/Chromium infrastructure. Building BrowserManager once serves both.
- Chose to defer the theme engine to Sprint 6 (after all element types are built) and use a hardcoded default theme in Sprint 2. This is logged as planned tech debt TD-001 / TD-A01.
- Chose to place TOC, cover page, and cross-references in Sprint 7 (not Sprint 2) because they depend on the full theme engine for proper styling and are P1, not P0.
- Chose to put Preview, multi-file, and diagnostics in Sprint 8 (final sprint) because they are integration-level features that depend on everything else working.
